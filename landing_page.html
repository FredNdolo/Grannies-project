Who We Are

Trefoil Guild Kenya is a vibrant branch of the Kenya Girl Guides Association. We foster lifelong commitment to the Guide Promise and Law, encouraging community service, mentorship, and continued engagement in Girl Guiding.

Members include Guide leaders who are not running Guide Units and are either pursuing studies or have changed jobs, alongside new associates aged 18 and above.

We actively support intergenerational mentorship, community development, and national initiatives aligned to WAGGGS values and the Sustainable Development Goals.

The Challenge

Why the Grannies Project Matters

Thousands of grandmothers across Nairobi's informal settlements are raising grandchildren alone — with no income, no support, and no voice. We exist to change that.

👵

Skipped-Generation Caregiving

These grandmothers have been left to care for grandchildren due to bereavement, abandonment, or social disruption — often with no warning and no resources.

🏘️

Vulnerability & Isolation

Mostly aged women living in informal settlements like Kibra in Nairobi, facing poverty, social isolation, and limited access to public services.

📉

Lack of Income & Support

Without income-generating skills or market access, they lack the means to provide for their grandchildren or maintain their own dignity and wellbeing.

What We Do

Our Key Activities

01

Skill Development

We focus on improving crafts quality and design through individualized, hands-on training sessions tailored to each grandmother's skill level and interests.

02

Material Access

Trefoil Guild Kenya provides affordable, quality materials for production — removing a key barrier to consistent and marketable output.

03

Market Linkages

We facilitate sales through local markets, online platforms, Trefoil Guild Members, and the Kenya Girl Guides Headquarters shop in Nairobi.

04

Financial Education

We provide training in basic financial literacy, pricing, and business management — giving grannies the knowledge to grow their craft into a sustainable livelihood.

05

Christmas Gift Baskets

Every year we bring joy to grannies and their grandchildren through curated Christmas gift baskets — a moment of care, community, and celebration.

06

Community Cohesion

Beyond the crafts, we build lasting social bonds — creating a network of mutual support, mentorship, and shared purpose among the grannies and the Guild.

Our Impact

What We've Achieved

Through persistent effort and community trust, the Grannies Project has produced measurable and lasting change in the lives of its beneficiaries.

✓

Increased Income

Project beneficiaries are earning more through improved craft production and access to new markets.

✓

Improved Product Quality

Training has raised the standard and marketability of craft items, opening doors to premium buyers and exhibitions.

✓

Established Market Linkages

Strong sales channels now connect grannies to customers locally, nationally, and online.

✓

Financial Literacy

Grannies now manage their own pricing, savings, and simple business operations with confidence.

✓

Strengthened Social Bonds

Community cohesion and mutual support networks have been rebuilt among previously isolated women.

✓

Diversified Craft Range

The range of craft items produced has grown — beadwork, woven baskets, crocheted goods, and more.
